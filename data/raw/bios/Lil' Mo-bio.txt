Lil' Mo (born Cynthia Loving in 1978 on Long Island, New York) is an R&B singer and songwriter. She is best known for collaborating with artists such as Ja Rule, Missy Elliott, and Fabolous, and for her hit single "Superwoman Pt. II". Lil' Mo's third album "Syndicated The Lil' Mo Show" was shelved by Cash Money Records dispite having four minor hit singles. She independently released her fourth  album "Pain & Paper" in 2007. It sold 101,000 copies to date. She is now signed to Global Music Group for a two album deal. Her fourth album "Tattoos & Roses" is to be a double album. One disc of live content and the other studio recorded content. Lil' Mo confirmed guest appearances by MC Lyte, Tweet, Fabolous and other performers she admires.
