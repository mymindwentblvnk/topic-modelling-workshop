There are (or: once were) at least two bands called Puncture.

1. Puncture was a short-lived British punk bank, established in the London area of Islington, United Kingdom, in 1976. They released one 7-inch single, 'You Can't Rock and Roll (In a Council Flat)', on Chiswick in September 1977, before splitting up in early 1978. Personnel: Paul McCallum (guitar, vocals), Jack Stafford (guitar, vocals), Steve Counsel (bass, vocals), Marty Truss (drums).
