Warren Ellis (born 14 February 1965, Ballarat, Victoria, Australia) is an Australian multi-instrumentalist and composer, most famous for his work with Dirty Three. He plays violin, keyboards and mandolin. He is currently also member of the bands Nick Cave and The Bad Seeds and Grinderman. 

Nick Cave (born 22 September 1957) is an Australian rock musician, songwriter, author, screenwriter and occasional actor, best known for his work with the Bad Seeds and earlier The Birthday Party. 

Cave and Ellis collaborated on several film scores. In 2005 on the award-winning score of The Proposition, the screenplay of which was written by Cave. In 2008 they did the Music From the Motion Picture The Assassination of Jesse James by the Coward Robert Ford, in which Cave made an appearance as a singer. And in 2009 for John Hillcoat's The Road.
