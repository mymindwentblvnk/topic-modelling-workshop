One of the most exciting exports from the tiny Sout American country is surely the music of the inspired and unpredictable Martín Buscaglia, or "the Uruguayan Prince"."El evangelio según mi jardinero" is a definition-defying Picasso of a pop canvas, that mixes deep funk with Spanish folk and flamenco, and crocodile-tear-jerking Latino love songs with squelchy synths and gay abandon. The hyper-kitsch cover of Minnie Riperton's "Loving you" doesn..t just work, it somehow seems inevitable
