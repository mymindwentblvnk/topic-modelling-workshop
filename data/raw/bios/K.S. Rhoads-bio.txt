K.S. Rhoads (born 7 August 1976 in Tucson, Arizona) is an American musician, singer-songwriter, composer, music-producer, and performer.

Rhoads is said to have bought his first 4 track recorder while in 9th grade and began recording his own songs. He moved to Nashville in 2001, but it was not until 2007 that he started work on his debut album Dead Language with producers Robin Eaton, and Lij Shaw.

Rhoads often plays piano and acts as music director for Universal recording artist Erin McCarley.

He has toured and recorded with fellow Nashville musicians for a project called Ten Out of Tenn.
